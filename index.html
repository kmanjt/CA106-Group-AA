Our   Cultures

Ireland

Ireland is located on the west side of Europe right beside the UK on its west side. Its population is just shy of 5 million people with numbers still rising ever since the end of the famine. The native language in Ireland is Irish although it is on the decline with English being the main language spoken for some time. Ireland is mainly a christian country but there are many other religions that are practised in Ireland.

Estonia

Located in north eastern Europe, Estonia has a population of around 1.3 million people. The official language is Estonian, however most people speak at least one other language as well. English and Russian would be most common but so too would be Finnish and Swedish. Romania

Romania is a beautiful, massive country located in eastern Europe with a rich and long history, with some of the earliest forms of European civilizations found there. It has a population of just over 19 million. In 1989, a CIA organized revolution led to the overthrowing of the communist dictatorship. Today, Romania is a key NATO ally and member of the European Union.

Moldova

Republic of Moldova is a small landlocked country located in Eastern Europe between Romania and Ukraine. The current population is around 2 million and the official language is Romanian. A big majority of the population can speak both Romanian and Russian. Moldovans are mostly religious, we follow Christianity, specifically Eastern Orthodox.

Nigeria

Nigeria is a West African country. It is located in western coast of Africa, with neighbouring countries such as Benin, Niger, Chad and Cameroon. It is a multicultural country with a huge population of around 200 million and over 350 unique tribes. Nigeria is one of the most linguistically diverse countries in the world! There are over 500 different languages spoken, however the official language is English. There are also many different religions practiced in Nigeria, the most popular being Islam and Christianity.

India

India is located in the south of Asia, between the Bay of Bengal and the Arabian sea. It consists of a population of 1.38 billion people. India can be considered one of the most diverse places on Earth due to how many different languages people speak. Every 10 km, the local language changes. The national language is Hindi, while over 19,500 local languages are spoken across India. Furthermore, India is a very religious place; the main religion that nearly 80% of its population follows Hinduism.
