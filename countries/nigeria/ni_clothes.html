    Clothing   &   Dances
    Nigeria

    Introduction - Clothing/Dances

    Traditional Nigerian clothing is very colourful. Nigerians use the umbrella term “native” for their traditional clothes. Traditional Nigerian clothes are filled with bright colours and beautiful patterns. Each tribe wears their own unique traditional clothes and have their own style. The most popular traditional Nigerian clothes are: Agbadas and geles.

    Agbada:

    The agbada is a wide long sleeve gown worn by men. The agbada is adopted from the Hausa people. It is formal attire and is often worn to different kinds of important ceremonies like parties and weddings. It is made of 3 different seperate pieces: a gown with very large openings at the sides, a long-sleeved shirt and a sokoto (a pair of trousers that are slim at the ankles). The agbada is embroidered with traditional patterns and is made of silk or cotton.

    (WorldAtlas, 2019)

    Gele:

    The geles is a decorative head piece worn by women. It can be worn daily, however, there are some grand styles that women wear to special occasions like weddings or important parties. Geles are often worn to church also. Geles are made with strong material which helps them keep their shape. Like almost everything else in Nigeria, each tribe (that wear geles) has their own style of tying them.

    (WorldAtlas, 2019)

    Esan Clothing

    I’m part of the Esan tribe. Esan people are from Edo state and dress in traditional Edo attire. Again, they usually wear these native clothes on special occasions. Edo people love beads, to them it represents freedom and royalty. Some other accessories they wear are anklets and bracelets.

    Dances

    Nigeria is rich with different types of dancing. There are hundreds of traditional dances. Modern and trendy Nigerian dances are very popular in pop culture nowadays. These dances are often danced to afrobeats (a music genre that’s a combination of west african musical styles, jazz and funk). However, some hip-hop dances are inspired by these Nigerian dances. An example of these dances are the Shaku Shaku and the Zanku.


    Shaku Shaku:
    Shaku shaku is a dance performed by crossing your arms in front of you at your wrists and taking gallop-like steps in a graceful manner. Much like a lot of Nigerian dances, the dance is mostly freestyle. The origin of the dance is quite controversial, some believe that it started in Agege (an area in Lagos). The dance became very popular in 2016 after the afrobeats songs “Shepeteri” and “Legbegbe” dropped.
    Zanku:
    Zanku is a dance performed by making ten-ten like marches and with an occasional kick called “gbeing body”. The dance was popularised in 2018 and again is often freestyled. A popular afrobeat song that the dance is often paired with is Chinko Ekun’s “Able God”.
    (Onyekwena, 2018; Sliver N, 2019)
