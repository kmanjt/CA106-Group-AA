    Nigeria

    Introduction - History

    Nigeria, the West African country, has a beautiful history. As of today, it is one of the most multicultural countries in the world with a population of over 200 million people, over 350 unique tribes and 500 different spoken languages! The country’s currency is Naira, represented with the symbol ₦. The country was named by a 19th century journalist called Flora Shaw, the name comes from the River Niger.
    Nigeria's history is rich. The country is rich in spices, metals and oils, and amazing artists, engineers, architects and metalworkers.

    (FTD Facts, 2017)

    The Nok

    The earliest record of civilization in Nigeria is the Nok Culture (1500 BC - 200 AD). The Nok had power over a region on the north-east side of the River Niger; this region was as big as modern-day France. They held such power because they were hundreds of years ahead of their neighbours. The Nok are known for their use of iron technology. They were smelting iron weapons as far back as 550 BC, and maybe even 1000 BC. As well as making beautiful and extremely intricate iron sculptures.
    Sadly, not much is known about The Nok. Their mining and metalworking techniques were passed down to their successors.
    Europeans stole a lot of Nok artefacts, and now they are scattered in museums in Europe, The US and Japan.

    (Epimetheus, 2018)

    Ancient City of Ife

    Next is the ancient Yoruba city of Ife (also known as; Ile Ife). According to the Yoruba religion, Ile Ife was created by the Supreme God, Olodumare. He ordered Obatala to create earth, but when Obatala found himself drunk on palm wine his younger brother, Oduduwa, took the opportunity to create things instead. This caused an ever-lasting conflict between Obatala and Oduduwa. Oduduwa is believed to be the ancestor of the first divine king, while Obatala is believed to have created the first Yoruba people out of clay.
    The word “Ife” means expansion and the word “Ile” in modern day Yoruba means house or home, so Ile-Ife means “The House of Expansion”.
    Again, similarly to the Nok, the Yoruba people from the ancient city were amazing metalworkers and sculptors. The city is famous for it’s amazing, realistic figures made of metal.

    (Wikipedia Contributors, 2021)

    The Nri Kingdom

    Lastly, The Kingdom of Nri. The kingdom dates back as far as the 1500s, where a god-like figure, Eri, settled in the area. Eri is described as a being that came from the sky, sent by Chukwu (God). The Kingdom of Nri was a medieval kingdom controlled by a high priest called an “Eze Nri”. The high priest controlled the trading and diplomacy of the kingdom. Nri is believed to be the heart and origin of the Igbos.
    For many centuries the Nri people lived peacefully because they believed violence was a disease that plagued the earth. The kingdom’s primary activities were hunting and agriculture. The kingdom was also a place for people who had been ostrasized and a safe haven for slaves.

    (BioGreat Tv, 2020a)
