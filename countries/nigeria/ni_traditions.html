    Nigeria

    Introduction - Traditions/Customs

    Nigeria is the most populous country for black people, and it’s the 7th most populous country in the world. It’s extremely multicultural with over 300 unique tribes and over 500 unique languages. Each tribe has its own customs and beliefs.

    Nigeria is a huge country with so many different types of people, it is insane. There are so many different languages spoken in Nigeria. 7% of all the world’s languages are spoken in Nigeria. For the most part, each tribe speaks their own language. The most common languages, and the largest tribes, are Yoruba, Igbo and Hausa.

    (FTD Facts, 2017)

    Quick Facts About The Popular Tribes

    Hausa

    1. The Hausa population is concentrated in the North part of Nigeria, between River Niger and Lake Chad.
    2. Hausa is the largest tribe in Nigeria, making up approximately 25% of the population.
    3. Hausa people preserved their culture, and still keep to their way of life from before British colonisation.
    4. Their main religion is Islam, which is believed to have been brought from traders in Mali during their trade exchanges.
    5. They speak Hausa.

    Yoruba

    1. Yoruba people can be found in the southwest and north central parts of Nigeria.
    2. Yoruba people can also be found in Neighbouring countries, such as Benin Republic and Togo.
    3. Yoruba is the 2nd largest tribe in Nigeria, making up approximately 21% of the population.
    4. Ile Ife (where, according to Yoruba mythology, life began) is considered the spiritual center for Yoruba people.
    5. Their main religions are Christianity and Islam, while a minority practise their traditional beliefs
    6. They speak Yoruba.

    Igbo

    1. The Igbo population is concentrated in the Southeast part of Nigeria
    2. Igbo is the 3rd largest tribe in Nigeria, making up approximately 18% of the population.
    3. Igbo people are descendants of the Nri Kingdom
    4. Igbo people have their traditions and beliefs but after colonisation a large majority converted to Christinaity.
    5. They speak Igbo
    (Okoh, 2018)

    My Tribe - Esan

    I, personally, am part of the Esan tribe.
    It is a minor tribe in Edo State. Its population is around 1.5 million people. Esan people are known for their agricultural skills. A majority of Esan people are Christians. Esan people speak many different dialects of the Esan language, which stems from Bini.
    I come from a family of farmers (on my mom's side). Her family produced an assortment of crops, for example; plantain, rice yam, cassava, maize, scotch bonnet and many more vegetables. My mom has told me many stories from when she was younger. When she was younger, she practised a religion called Ogun. Ogun is the god of Iron. She had a small shrine in her house for iron. Her father would leave offerings. However, it wasn't long before her family was introduced to Christanity, so for a huge majority of her life she’s been a practising Christian.

    Tribal Marks

    Tribal marks are a huge part of Nigerian culture. It’s a sign of beauty and identity. It’s usually done when you are a child. Using a knife, cuts are made to the face, then sediment is rubbed into the cut so it can heal open and leave a scar. Each tribe has a specific tribal mark, some of the larger tribes had many variations of their tribal marks. With the introduction of Abrehamic religions in Nigeria, tribal marks have become a lot less popular.
