    Background & History
    Moldova

    Background

    Republic of Moldova, or simply knowns as Moldova, is a small country located in Eastern Europe between Romania and Ukraine. Moldova was influenced by many nations throughout its history, starting with the invasions of the Roman Empire, Ottoman Empire, Byzantine Empire, and many other nations from Asia, due to its strategical location between western Europe and Asia.

    History

    Pre 20th Century


    From 14th to 19th century, Moldova was part of the Principality of Moldavia, stretching from the Carpathian Mountains to the Dniester River and the Black Sea. In 1812, Moldova was annexed by the Russian Empire and became Bessarabia (Basarabia). The Russian Empire authorities incentivised other ethnicities to colonise Moldova in order for it to lose its ethnic value and nationalism and make it easier for them to Russificate our nation.


    20th Century - Unification, break-up and Soviet Era


    In 1917, during the Russian Revolution and the political instability in the Russian Empire, Bessarabia found the opportunity to proclaim it’s independence from Russia and to unite with the Kingdom of Romania. In 1918 on the 1st of December all the Romanian Nations - Bukovina, Transylvania, Bessarabia, and Walachia united under a single country - The Kingdom of Romania, or also known as Big Romania (România Mare).

    Throughout history, many leaders and kings tried to unite all of the Romanian lands together but it was a very difficult task to achieve. Before 1918, it was done only once before and it didn’t even last a year. This is why for all Romanians and Moldovians alike the unification of our countries means a lot to our patriotic status as we were always the siblings who shared so much in common but were always separated, because of other empires who had stronger political power than us.

    During the Second World War, Soviet Union got back Bessarabia and until 1991 Moldova was part of the Soviet Union. During this time a lot of anti-Romanian propaganda was pushed in order to make people forget about their ethical identities. They made up a Slavicised alphabet for the Romanian Language and called it The Moldovian Language. According to (Wikipedia, 2021) , Soviet Union invested a lot in Moldova to develop industrial and scientific facilities. It was probably the best period to be living in as everything was booming and there was some social and economic security, that’s if we ignore all the censorships and harsh rules.


    After the Soviet Union broke up, along with other Soviet Union satellite states, Moldova proclaimed its independence in 1991 and since then we are trying to grow out of the ideologies which persevered in Soviet Union and slowly rebuild our country to its best self.
