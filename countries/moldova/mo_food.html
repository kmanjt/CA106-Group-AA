    Moldova

    Introduction to Moldovian Foods

    Moldovian cuisine is influenced by Romanian, Russian, Ukrainian, Polish, and Greek cuisines. We have a lot of meat in our food with many vegetables and a lot of dairy products. Moldova has very fertile soil which allows us to grow many vegetables and domestic animals.Climatic conditions and fertile soil also allows us to grow tasty grapes with which we make homemade wine.

    Mămăliga

    The most popular Moldovian Traditional dish is also a well-known Romanian dish - Mămăligă(Polenta). It is usually served with other side dishes such as traditional pork or beef stews - friptură, with some homemade cottage cheese and sour cream. We also have a dipping sauce, called mujdei de usturoi, made from garlic, water, and salt, sometimes vinegar too. The way this dish is served is we usually take a small bit of mămăligă, dip it in sour cream or mujdei de usturoi, and have some stew after. Delicious!

    Zeamă

    Zeamă is my favorite dish. It is a traditional Moldovian chicken clear soup. It is very similar to chicken noodle soups but we usually add something sour at the end to give it a nice kick. The ingredients are chicken, some chopped vegetables - usually the most important ones are onions, potatoes, carrots, and peppers; and homemade noodles - tăiței. To make it sour we add borș acru which is fermented wheat. Recently people started using lemons which gives it a nice taste too.

    Sărmale

    Like any other country in Eastern Europe and the Balkans - stuffed cabbage rolls are always included in traditional holidays. In Moldova, we call them sărmale. We usually use pickled cabbage in which we will add and roll a stuffing with rice, minced chicken or pork, with some carrots and onions. Once we have our little cabbage rolls we let them slow cook for a couple of hours and once they’re done we dip them in sour cream and enjoy the delicious taste.

    Plăcinte

    We also like our traditional pies. Pies showcase the baker’s skill and finesse and if done well - your taste buds will scream in joy. The most popular pies are cottage cheese pies, cabbage pies, potato pies, and pumpkin pies. Our pies have a specific way in which they are presented, giving them a unique and beautiful look.
