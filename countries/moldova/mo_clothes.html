    Traditional Clothing & Dances
    Moldova

    Traditional Clothing

    The traditional Moldovan costume is a piece of art that showcases the beautiful skill of our women and their artistic visualisations. It’s a precious testimony for the beauty of our culture, a process that dates back centuries and that was a very important aspect of our social life.

    It wasn’t tolerable to duplicate and copy the same ornamental motifs from another person's costume. Every woman and girl had to create their own costumes, showcasing the importance of local traditions and their artistry. The costume is usually made to relate to the person - their character, eye and hair color, age, and social status.


    Each costume was unique. It is very difficult to find two costumes that had exactly the same motifs and colors. Every person had one or several costumes, all differing from each other in part in design and color, and proportions. Making men's embroidered shirts was the same meticulous process - unique motifs, designs and colors, with great attention to detail. They wore shirts embroidered by mothers, grandparents or future brides, all with unique designs. However, in modern times, it would be too expensive to have unique costumes, so this rule is not valid anymore.

    The materials used to make these costumes were mostly homemade fabrics. They were usually made from wool, linen, cotton, hemp, or silk, and they remained unchanged for hundreds of years. Making beautiful home decorations from homemade materials is also a big part of our culture - embroidering pillows, making traditional rugs, curtains, etc.

    Traditional Dances

    Moldovan folk dance is one of the oldest folk arts. Each movement speaks of the talent and spiritual characteristics of the people in different historical periods. They highlight both the character and the temperament, strength, and sharpness of the people, highlight the wisdom and humor, sincerely and directly reveal the aspirations and feelings of Moldovians.

    According to the form, structure, character, the traditional dances from Moldova are part of the great and rich family of Balkan-Carpathian choreography, unitary through the execution of movements as a collective and group game. The characteristic of the Moldovian dance is the parallel movement of the knees and toes.

    The analysis of dance characteristics in the Republic of Moldova led to their delimitation in four areas: southern, central, northern and the left bank of the Dniester, in dozens of variants, with small changes, improvisations, characteristic features, found in songs, rhythm , tempo, shouts, and chirping. To these are added other dances, assimilated from the traditional culture of other people.

    The sources of traditional Moldovian dances come from the Thracians and Geto-Dacians. Several ritual customs in antiquity are related to entering a specific state through dance. Among the most famous Moldovan ritual dances can be mentioned Căluşarii and Drăgaica. They have deep roots in the history of our people's culture. From ancient times the daily life of the people has been accompanied by community dances, which reflected with great power the emotional energy of Moldovians, such as Hora, Sârba, Bătuta, Brâul, Roata, Trei Lemne.
