    Customs & Traditions
    Moldova

    Short Introduction - Traditions

    Moldovian culture is very rich in traditions and customs. Current traditions are a mix between old geto-daci cultures and more recent Russian, Greek and Roman influences. Our traditions usually include crafts, wine, food, music, dances, holidays, arts and motifs.

    Mărțișor

    One of the cutest traditions we have is ‘Mărțișor’ (Martsishor). The legend of the Mărțișor says that when seasons change from Winter to Spring, Winter doesn’t want to allow Spring to take the rule for the seasons so she summons strong winds trying to make Spring go away. A thorn stings Spring and a drop of red blood falls on a snowdrop below so one of its petals is red, and the other one is white.

    A Mărțișor is usually a brooch worn near the heart and it has a white piece and a red one. The tradition is to gift people a Mărțișor on the first of March, symbolising the victory of Spring over Winter. We usually wear the Mărțișor throughout March and at the end of the month, we attach them to trees and make a wish. We consider that those wishes will come true and also that the trees will have plenty of fruit throughout the year.

    Pottery

    Moldova also has a rich and ancient history in making distinguished ceramics items and decorations. We use dots, lines, and circles designs which makes a unique design. Some of the most popular ceramic items we have in Moldova are oala, ulcior or burlui. They are used to cook traditional food and to serve homemade wine.

    Wine

    Winemaking is also an important aspect of Moldovian village life. Due to favorable climatic conditions, close proximity to Greece, and early influences of the Roman Empire, winemaking became one of our most valued traditions. In every village household, there is a cellar specially made to keep wine during winter with other goods. You would usually find wooden barrels filled with homemade wine in these cellars. It is a custom that whenever the family has guests they serve them wine which is a symbolic drink used to honor the host and start a nice dinner.

    Christmas Eve Carols

    On Christmas Eve, kids and teenagers go from door to door to sing carols about Christmas and New Year in traditional clothing. It is considered that these carols drive away evil spirits. The groups of carolers are greeted by friendly hosts, who have beautifully decorated houses, with fir branches and who offer guests fruit, pretzels, sweets, and even money. Nowadays, the habit of going with carols is kept unaltered, especially in the countryside, but also in some cities children go and sing carols at the homes of friends or neighbors.

    Easter Traditions

    Easter traditions are more numerous. On Easter Eve, housewives paint the eggs - the traditional color being red, then bake a specific cake we call ‘cozonac’. On the night of the Lord's resurrection, family members go to the consecration of these specific dishes. And in the morning after consecration, everyone washes their cheeks with a red egg and a white one, which symbolizes power and purity, this "ritual" being followed by a meal at which the whole family celebrates together.
