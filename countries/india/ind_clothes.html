    Women's Clothes






    There are different traditional outfits, but we will only be going through the main key ones.

    In Sanskrit, the word ' sari ' means ' strip of cloth '. It is a traditional dress for women in India. The ' strip of cloth ' is usually 4.5 to 9 metres. There are a variety of ways to wrap the cloth. Usually, it's wrapped around the waist a couple of times before getting draped over the shoulder. The earliest a sari has been worn in history is as early as the Indus Valley civilization. Due to their hot climate and need for flexibility, Indian women prefer sari made of cotton. Nearly 75% of Indian women wear a sari every day.

    Lehenga Choli's origins go back to Gujarat, a state in India located in the northwest of India. The outfit consists of a long skirt paired with choli and a blouse fitted at the waist. It is usually made with very bright colours; its designs date back to the Mogul Culture. The outfit is mainly worn at weddings and festivals. It is often accompanied by a chunri, a long transparent cloth bordered with lace, similar to a veil.

    Salwar Kameez/ churidar originated in Punjab and Kashmir as a more comfortable choice of wear for Indian women. It is the most commonly worn female dress in India due to its comfort. The outfit is compared to salwar trousers, which is tight at the ankles. Salwar can be paired with a kameez, a long loose dress worn over the salwar. A churidar may be worn if needed.

    Men's Clothes






    A garment traditionally worn by men at home is known as Dhoti. It is a long garment, around 4.5 metres, tired with a knot at the waist and usually paired with a kurta. In the south, Dhoti can also pair it with angavastram. Dhotis are used in both formal and informal wear. Mahatma Gandhi wore a dhoti to public occasions and popularized the outfit.

    Achkan is a traditional jacket usually stretched down to the knees. It is the outfit they are required to wear for medieval court for men, but it was involved into more of casual wear. It usually had a front and side opening which was tied by strings. It is mainly worn over a churidar or Dhoti. This outfit is also used in the formal and informal environment, although it is more commonly seen in weddings.
