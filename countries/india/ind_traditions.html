    Traditions








    Most of the beliefs India has are rooted in its religion as Indian is a very religious country with over 80% of people being part of the Hindu religion.

    I'm sure you must have heard of the famous Hindi greeting, ' namaste '. It is used to say hello and goodbye. Sanskrit directly translates to ' i bow to the divine in you '. The gesture is made by putting your palms together in the front of your chest and turning slightly.

    Most families in India are joint families where the entire family all live together, including the parents, wife, children, and relatives. the head of the family is usually a senior adult who makes decisions on behalf of the family. Incomes typically go into a shared poo, which benefits all members. These days there has been an increase in nuclear-like families than joint families due to improved economic development and urbanisation.

    Customs

    While Hinduism is the main religion in Indian, many other religions in India co-exist together. The cow is considered to be a sacred animal in Hinduism. Each part of the cow symbolises a mythological story of god in Hinduism. for example, the four legs stand for the four objectives in life 0 desire, material wealth, righteousness and salvation. Due to this, eating or even killing a cow is considered sinful and against the law.

    Fasts are essential and frequent in India; they are thought that you are cleansing yourself of all the sins you have committed since you last had a fast by going with the food. Fasts are there to thank the gods and goddesses and show their appreciation.
