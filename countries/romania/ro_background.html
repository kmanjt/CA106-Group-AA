    Romania

    Background

    Romania is a country in southeastern Europe. The name ‘Romania’ comes from the colloquial “român” (pronounced Rom - un), which comes from the Latin “romanus”, meaning a person of Rome.

    • Today, the capital of Romania is Bucharest. Romania is a country with a rich history revolving heavily around military conquests and warfare (primarily being the victim of such invasions).


    Romania is the host country of amongst the oldest homo sapien remains found in Europe, at 34,950 years old. The earliest European civilization was also found in Romania. It is believed, however heavily disputed scientifically, that the oldest known evidence of human writing was found in Romania.

    • These discoveries are known as the Tărtăria tablets. If genuine, their date exceeds the range available via carbon dating (50,000 years).


    Suffice it to say, Romania is both a present and historical hotbed of human activity due to its geographical position in Europe. Present day Romania was known as Dacia by the ancient Greeks and later the Romans. Dacia was inhabited by tribes known as Thracians and later, Dacians. The symbol or ensign of the Dacians is known as the ‘Dacian Draco’. It is a wolf with open jaws and the body of a serpent-like dragon.

    History

    Dacia became Roman Dacia and was ruled by the Roman Empire from 106-275 AD. Its’ natural resources were heavily exploited by the Roman empire, which primarily included large deposits of gold. Romania was a heavily colonized province in the Roman Empire and vulgar Latin was adopted by the native peoples, ultimately becoming the only Eastern European Romance language today, Romanian.
