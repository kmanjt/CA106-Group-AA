    Estonian   Foods

    Christmas Dinner

    Christmas in Estonia is celebrated on the 24th of December. Christmas Dinner is very unique and special. It involves the key element blood sausage along with sauerkraut, pickled pumpkin salad, lingonberry jam, oven roasted potatoes, pork and sült which is best described as 'meat jelly'.

    Potato Salad



    Potato salad is one of the most typical Estonian foods. It is found on every table no matter the occasion. It mainly consists of boiled potatoes, cucumber, pickles, apples, egg, peas, meat, mayonaise and creme freiche.

    Rosolje is the beetroot equivalent. The texture is the same but after being left for a few hours the beetroot stains the salad and gives it a beautiful pink colour.

    Herring Sandwiches

    In Estonia different there is two names under which bread may be classified. The first is 'Leib' which is fermented rye bread or simply black bread and the second is 'Sai' which is plain wheat bread or white bread.

    Leib is eaten with almost every meal. 'Küüslauguleivad' are a popular snack eaten in pubs or restaurants. They are pieces of leib with garlic often dipped in a creme freiche dip.

    Bread and fish are both staples in every Estonian's diet. Herring and sprat on leib are a classic starter meal. They are often served with onion and dill.

    Kama


    Kama is a mixture of roasted barley, rye, oat and pea flour. It has the texture of a powder. Kama can be mixed with kefir and sugar or made into a dessert with berries.
