    Background


    Dating back to as early as the 13th century Estonia has been under the control of many countries including Denmark, Sweden, Germany, Poland and of course Russia. On the 24th of February 1918 Estonia declared itself an independent and democratic state.




    The Raeapteek Pharmacy is one of the oldest running pharmacies in Europe. Next year will mark it being open for 600 years!

    History


    Whilst Russia was in power everything Estonian or anti Soviet Union was censored. You were put in jail if you had an Estonian flag and children weren't even allow to draw it. Everything had to be approved by Russia, even song lyrics. The song below was translated into Russian as "What will we bring to the birthday?, Of course we'll bring, bring, bring". However the word 'viin' that means bring has two meanings, the other is 'vodka'. Quickly this song became a hit as it passed the Russian check.




    However, Estonia achieved real independence on the 20th of August 1991 after the Soviet Union declared so.

    On May 1st 2004 Estonia joined the European Union.
